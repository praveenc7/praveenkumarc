P / 02

Query Resource Isolation

Solving the "noisy neighbor" problem in Pinot
Co-architect & lead implementer

In a shared database, one heavy workload can slow down everyone else — the classic "noisy neighbor" problem. QRI gives each workload its own CPU and memory budget inside Pinot, so a single expensive query can't drag down the rest. That keeps latencies stable under heavy or unpredictable traffic, protecting product and site health.

It also drives efficiency: the same per-workload accounting powers a capacity estimator that sizes clusters more accurately and lets teams safely consolidate workloads, instead of over-provisioning to stay safe.

P / 03

Predictable Latencies

Page-cache warmup for Pinot
Owner · end-to-end

Pinot memory-maps its segments, so a freshly restarted host starts with a cold page cache. Early queries faulted data in from disk, causing large short-lived latency spikes, timeouts around deploys and refreshes, and repeated SLO breaches on high-traffic, site-facing tables.

I designed and shipped a query-driven warmup framework. An offline pipeline analyzes historical logs to pick representative queries per table; at startup or after a segment refresh, the host runs them at a controlled rate before accepting live traffic. Per-table settings and hard timeouts keep it safe — if warmup is missing or slow, the server comes online normally.
